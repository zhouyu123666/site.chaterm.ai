1.What data do we collect?

We collect personal data relating to you (“Personal Data”) as follows:

(a) Personal Data that you voluntarily provide to us: We collect Personal Data if you use our Services or communicate with us as follows:

Login Options:You may access our Services by signing in through your e-mail , iOS account or Google account. When using these third-party login methods, we only receive basic profile information (e.g., username or email address) required for authentication, as permitted by the respective platform.

User Content: In order to provide Chaterm DevOps Services, we collect server host IP address, port, and login password that you provide to our Services. The Personal Data we collect cannot be used for identifying a specific person. When the service is finished or upon the closing of the application, the personal data are discarded.

Contact information. We may record your e-mail address when you contact us for service support, making a complaint, suggestion or feedback to us by sending emails to help us keep contact with you for updates and support to resolve your queries for use of our Services.

Collection of Voice Content: During your use of voice input, we process audio data captured by the microphone in real-time. This audio data contains the speech content of your utterances, which is converted into text or directly used as commands to engage the large language model for analysis and to generate corresponding feedback for you. The core of our collection and processing is the linguistic information contained within the speech, aimed at understanding your intent and providing the service

Important Clarification: No Collection of Voiceprint Information: We expressly state that in this process, we do not intentionally identify, extract, or collect any voiceprint information(e.g., information reflecting the physical characteristics of your voice) that could be used to identify your unique biological characteristics. Our system is designed to process the language content itself, not to analyze the individual characteristics of the voice.

(b) Personal Data We Receive from Your Use of the Services: When you visit, use, or interact with the Services, we receive the following information about your visit, use, or interactions (“Technical Information”):

Device data. To ensure the basic security of your account, network access and services, we need to collect your unique device identifiers (Android ID, IDFV,OAID), country, city, IP address, network access method, network quality data, and obtain Chaterm process information.

Usage Data: We collect information about your use of the Services, such as the types of content that you view or engage with, the features you use and the actions you take, as well as your time zone, country, the dates and times of access.

Access to Device Permissions. To receive your voice commands, this feature requires access to and use of the microphone permission on your device.

When you use the AI command completion and suggestion features, we will collect and store your frequently used command history in server operation scenarios (including but not limited to command line statements, operation parameters, and execution log summaries). Such information is only used to remember your operation habits across sessions, enable quick command invocation, grammar error correction, scenario-based recommendations and troubleshooting assistance, and improve server management efficiency. You may disable this feature via Settings - Extensions by turning off automatic completion.

12.Additional Notice for EU/EEA Residents(How We Comply with GDPR)

You have many rights relating to your personal information. Additional information on your rights can be requested directly from the data protection regulator within your country. Your rights include:

You have the right to object to certain types of processing, including processing for direct marketing.

You have the right to request access to your information and certain other information relevant to how your information is attained and used.

(d)The right to rectification

You are entitled to have your information corrected or amended if it is inaccurate or incomplete.

(e)The right to erasure

This right, also known as ”the right to be forgotten,” allows you to request that your information be permanently deleted or removed if you feel that you no longer want us to keep and use it. There can be exceptions to this dependent upon the situation.

(f)The right to restrict processing

You have the right to block or suppress the further use of your information. When processing is restricted, we can still store your information, but may not use it further. We keep lists of people who have asked for further use of their information to be blocked to make sure the restriction is respected in future.

(g)The right to data portability

You have the right to obtain and reuse your personal information for your own purposes across different services. For example, if you decide to switch to a new provider, this enables you to move, copy or transfer your information easily between our information technology systems and theirs safely and securely, without affecting its usability.

(h)The right to lodge a complaint

You have the right to lodge a complaint about the way we handle or process your personal information with your national data protection regulator.

(i)The right to withdraw consent

If you have given your consent to anything we do with your personal information, you have the right to withdraw your consent at any time (although if you do so, it does not mean that anything we have done with your personal information with your consent up to that point is unlawful). This includes your right to withdraw consent to us to use your personal information for marketing purposes.

If you make a request regarding your rights, we have one month to respond to you. If you would like to exercise any of the above rights, you can contact us via email at support@chaterm.ai.

Legal basis of processing your personal information

Besides obtaining your consent, we may process information if one of the following applies:

b. Necessary for the legitimate interests of us, where these are not overridden by your interests or fundamental rights and freedoms (Art 6(1)(f) of the GDPR).

13.Additional Notice for Brazilian Residents(How We Comply with LGPD)

The Brazilian Privacy statute, Lei Geral de Proteção de Dados (LGPD), enhances privacy rights and consumer protection for individuals who reside in Brazil. We are committed to full LGPD compliance.

The grounds on which we process your personal information

We can process your personal information only if we have a legal basis to do so. The following legal bases are recognized under the LGPD:

Your consent to processing activities;

Our compliance with a legal and regulatory requirements;

Carrying out of public policies provided in laws or regulations or based on contracts, agreements and similar legal instruments;

Carrying out of a contract and its preliminary procedures, in cases where you are a party to said contract;

Exercising of our rights in judicial, administrative or arbitration procedures;

Protection or physical safety of yourself or a third party; and

Our legitimate interests, provided that your fundamental rights and liberties do not prevail over such interests.

Your Brazilian privacy rights

You have the right to:

(a)obtain confirmation of the existence of processing activities on your personal information;

(b)access your personal information;

(c)have incomplete, inaccurate or outdated personal information rectified;

(d)obtain the anonymization, blocking or elimination of your unnecessary or excessive personal information including information that is not being processed in compliance with the LGPD;

(e) obtain information on the options to provide or deny your consent and the consequences thereof;

(f) obtain information about the third parties with whom we share your personal information;

(g) obtain, upon your express request, the portability of your personal information (except for anonymized information) to another service or product provider, provided that our commercial and industrial secrets are safeguarded;

(h) obtain the deletion of your personal information being processed if the processing was based upon your consent, unless one or more exceptions provided for in Article 16 of the LGPD apply;

(i) revoke your consent at any time;

(j) file a complaint related to your personal information with the Brazilan National Data Protection Authority (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados or “ANPD”);

(k)oppose a processing activity in cases where the processing is not carried out in compliance with the law;

(l)request clear and adequate information regarding the criteria and procedures used for an automated decision; and

(m)request the review of decisions made solely on the basis of the automated processing of your personal information, which affect your interests, if applicable.

You will never be discriminated against, or otherwise suffer any sort of detriment, if you exercise your rights.

How to file your request

You can file your express request to exercise your rights free from any charge, at any time, by using the contact details provided in this Privacy Policy, or via your legal representative.

Transfer of personal information outside of Brazil permitted by the law

We are allowed to transfer your personal information outside of the Brazilian territory in the following cases:

(a)when the transfer is necessary for international legal cooperation between public intelligence, investigation and prosecution bodies, according to the legal means provided by the international law;

(b)when the transfer is necessary to protect your life or physical security or those of a third party;

(c)when the transfer is authorized by the Brazilan National Data Protection Authority (Autoridade Nacional de Proteção de Dados or “ANPD”);;

(d)when the transfer results from a commitment undertaken in an international cooperation agreement;

(e)when the transfer is necessary for the execution of a public policy or legal attribution of public service;

(f)when the transfer is necessary for compliance with a legal or regulatory obligation, the carrying out of a contract or preliminary procedures related to a contract, or the regular exercise of rights in judicial, administrative or arbitration procedures.
